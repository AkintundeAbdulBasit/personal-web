10 Essential Commands for Dog Training

" Welcome to our comprehensive guide to dog training! Teaching your fury friend essential commands can strengthen your bond, improve behaviour,and ensure their safety.In this article, we will explore the top 10 commands every dog owner should know,Get ready to unleash your dogs full potential!"

  • sit

    Purpose: Encourage your dog to sit on their haunches

    Usage: Before meals,during training or when meeting new people

    Training tips: Start with the short distances and gradually increase

  • stay

    Purpose:Teach your dog to remain stationary

    Usage: In various situations, like leaving the house or meeting other pets.

    Training tips: Start with the short distances and gradually increase

  • come

    Purpose: Recall your dog from any location

    Usage:During walks,emergencies or playtime

    Training tips: Use positive reinforcement amd consistent recall cues.

  • Down

    Purpose: Encourage your dog to lie down

    Usage: For relaxation,training or bedtime

    Training tips: Lure your dog inti a downward position with treats

  • Heel

    Purpose: Walk your dog beside you

    Usage: During walks or outings

    Training tips: Reward your dog for staying by your left side

  • leave

    Purpose: Teach your dog to ignore distractions

    Usage: With food,toys or other temptatios

    Training tips: Place a treat in front of your dog and say " leave it"

  • Drop

    Purpose: Release items from their mouth

    Usage: when your dog has something unwanted

    Training tips: Offer a trade or reward for releasing it

  • Roll Over

    Purpose:Fun tricks and bonding exercise

    Usage:During playtime or training

    Training tips: Start with "sit" and "dowm" positions

  • shake

    Purpose: Teach your dog to shake hands

    Usage: For socialization and greetings

    Training tips: Hold a treat and move it towards their paws

  • wait

    Purpose: Pause before entering rooms or recieving food

    Usage: Before meals ,walks or car rides

    Training tips: Stand still and reward patience
